Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most important things I should know about riding a bike?

Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.

How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a new one?

The one steadfast rule when it comes to helmets: regardless of wear, if you crash while wearing a helmet, replace it. Cycling helmets are single-use safety devices. Once you compress the foam underneath the helmet's plastic shell, it can no longer protect you.

My bike has been in storage is it safe to ride?

A bike that hasn't been ridden in three years might be safe to ride but you'll want to do an inspection prior to throwing a leg over it. Start with the frame. Make sure there isn't any corrosion that might lead to failure. Bike frames are pretty stout, so it will likely be okay.
